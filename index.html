Here is a rough timeline of money:

  • ca. 9000 BCE Grain and cattle were used as barter
  • ca. 3000 BCE The shekel was an ancient unit used in Mesopotamia
  • ca. 2050 BCE Code of Ur-Nammu, formalised the role of money in Mesopotamia
  • ca. 1760 BCE Code of Hammurabi, formalised the role of money in ancient Babylon
  • ca. 1500 BCE Money boxes in the Mycenae tombs
  • ca. 1000 BCE Money in the shape of small knives and spades made of bronze were in use in China during the Zhou dynasty
  • ca. 700 BCE First manufactured coins seems to have taken place separately in India, China, and in cities around the Aegean
  • ca. 650 BCE Other coins made of Electrum (a naturally occurring alloy of silver and gold) were manufactured on a larger scale in Lydia (on the coast of what is now Turkey)
  • ca. 7th C BCE Minting occurred amongst the cities of Grecian Asia Minor, spreading to Aegean parts of the Greek islands and the
  • ca. 500 BCE Minting occurred in the south of Italy
  • ca. 5th C BCE The Athenian empire used and exported silver coinage
  • 756 Silver penny introduced by King Offa of Mercia and later became the GDP
  • 11th C Paper money was introduced in Song Dynasty, China
  • 12th C The English monarchy introduced an early version of the bill of exchange in the form of a notched piece of wood known as a tally stick.
  • 13th C Paper money became known in Europe.
  • 14th C Europe had converted from use of silver in currency to minting of gold
  • 18th C Bills of exchange became a form of credit in England
  • 1792 The USD was first defined by the Coinage Act
  • 1st of January, 2002 Physical introduction of the Euro
  • 3rd of January 2009 Bitcoin

More information can be found on Wikipedia's The History of Money
